Overview of Gun Violence in America

Gun violence in America remains a critical issue that disproportionately affects marginalized and underprivileged communities, intensifying cycles of poverty and systemic injustice. As advocates for change, it is our duty to address the root causes of violence, including economic disparities, mental health, and access to firearms. We recognize the necessity of protecting individual freedoms, yet we must also demand comprehensive and sensible gun regulations that safeguard the rights of all citizens to live in safety. By focusing on preventative measures, such as enhancing background checks, supporting mental health initiatives, and community-based intervention programs, we can work towards a more equitable society where public safety and personal liberties are mutually respected. This balanced approach not only acknowledges the complexities surrounding gun rights but also actively seeks solutions that honor our collective responsibility to foster a peaceful and just nation.

Statistics

A majority of Americans (61%) say it is too easy to legally obtain a gun in this country. Another 30% say the ease of legally obtaining a gun is about right, and 9% say it is too hard to get a gun. Non-gun owners are nearly twice as likely as gun owners to say it is too easy to legally obtain a gun (73% vs. 38%). Meanwhile, gun owners are more than twice as likely as non-owners to say the ease of obtaining a gun is about right (48% vs. 20%).
